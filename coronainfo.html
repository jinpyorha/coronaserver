#COVID-19

 COVID-19 summary

The virus has been named “SARS-CoV-2” and the disease it causes has been named "coronavirus disease 2019” (abbreviated “COVID-19”). On March 11, COVID-19 outbreak was characerized as a pandemic by WHO source

Why should we take precaution?
  • COVID-19 transmits faster than other diseases (~twice+ as fast) source
  • COVID-19 can severely impair your respiratory system and more source
  • There is NO vaccine to combat it, nor any approved therapeutics
  • Although people of younger ages suffer milder symptoms, COVID-19 has demonstrated global case fatality rate of ~3.5% (as of March 9th) source
 How it spreads source

  • Between people in close contact with one another (within about 6 feet)
  • Through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes
  • It may be possible that a person can get COVID-19 by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it and then touching their own mouth, nose, or possibly their eyes, but this is not thought to be the main way the virus spreads

 Self-quarantine source

Stay home for 14 days from the time you left an area with widespread, ongoing community spread (Level 3 Travel Health Notice countries) and practice social distancing. Take the following steps:

  • Take your temperature 2+ times a day and monitor for fever. Watch for cough or trouble breathing
  • Stay home and avoid contact with others for the 14-day period
  • Do not take public transportation (you should not be going outside during this period!)

 Stopping COVID-19 source
#socialdistancing

  • Be aware of what's going on (it's more serious that you think!)
  • Wash your hands and practice good cough & sneeze etiquettes
  • Try to avoid gathering in public places--stay home as much as possible
  • Try not to use public transportation, going to grocery stores and gym when crowded
  • It's OK to postpone parties / public events
  • If you can work from home, work from home. Don't think of WFH as a vacation and travel

 What to do source

  • Stock up on supplies
  • Take everyday precautions to keep space between yourself and others Click to expand
    • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
    • Use hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol
    • Avoid touching high-touch surfaces in public places – elevator buttons, door handles, handrails, handshaking with people, etc. Use a tissue or your sleeve if you must touch something
    • Avoid touching your face, nose, eyes, etc.
    • Clean and disinfect your home to remove germs
    • Avoid crowds! The risk of exposure to respiratory viruses may increase in crowded, closed-in settings with little air circulation
    • Avoid all non-essential travel
  • When you go out in public, keep away from others who are sick, limit close contact and wash your hands often
  • Avoid crowds as much as possible
  • Avoid cruise and non-essential air travel
  • During a COVID-19 outbreak in your community, stay home as much as possible to further reduce your risk of being exposed

 Symptons*source

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Fever, cough
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion or inability to arouse
  • Bluish lips or face

*This list is not all inclusive. Please consult your medical provider for any other symptoms that are severe or concerning
